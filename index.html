places

In Kyrgyzstan, there are only 8 state reserves and 9 natural national parks. We will introduce you to the most unique places in Kyrgyzstan that are worth visiting this summer.

Parks

Issyk-Kul Biosphere Reserve

In 2000, the entire Issyk-Kul region was recognized as part of the UNESCO World Biosphere Network and is part of the Man and the Biosphere program. This reserve occupies 43,100 sq. km, that is, about 22% of the total area of ​​the territory of Kyrgyzstan. The reserve presents a wide variety of ecosystems, from deserts to mountains and swamps. Also, some species of endangered animals live here: Marco Polo sheep, Siberian stone goat,Tien Shan brown bear and snow leopard. Of particular interest in this biosphere reserve is how people interact with nature, as the area is a popular tourist destination and the higher mountains are used as summer pastures. One of the goals of the reserve is to promote sustainable development, which helps to protect the environment even in a thriving economy.

Ala-Archa National Park

Founded in 1974 to preserve the Ala-Archa River and the inhabitants of the gorge. On the territory of the park there are famous peaks and glaciers, including Mount Komsomolets (climbing it takes place every year on May 1) and Semenov Tienshansky Peak; as well as a hunting lodge and a climbers' cemetery. Ala-Archa Park, located just 35 km from Bishkek, is a popular place for picnics and mountain lovers. The name comes from the Kyrgyz "archa" - the name of a plant that the Kyrgyz burn to rid the house of evil spirits.

Besh-Aral State Reserve

Located far in the southwest of the Jalal-Abad region on the border with Uzbekistan, was founded in 1979 to preserve the natural forests of the Chatkal ridge, as well as to preserve the habitat of the endangered marmot Menzbir, as well as Greig and Kaufman tulips.

Besh-Tash State Natural National Park

On the territory of the park there is a river and lake Besh-Tash, as well as forests that are unique in their beauty. The reserve was established in 1996.

Chon-Kemin

Founded in 1997 in the valley of the Chon-Kemin river and includes almost all forestries in the Kemin district. The park was created to preserve the unique flora and fauna of these places. The park also includes hunting and botanical reserves and the mausoleum of Shabdan Batyr.

Issyk-Kul Reserve

Was the first specially protected natural area in Kyrgyzstan since 1941, is now considered the largest biosphere reserve in Kyrgyzstan. The reserve was created to protect unique species of animals and birds wintering near the shores of Lake Issyk-Kul. The protected area covers 19,000 hectares.
